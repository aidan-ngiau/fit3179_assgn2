UFO Data Dashboard

Unidentified Flying Objects (UFOs), have captured the imaginations of people worldwide.
In this dashboard, we will analyse UFO sighting data over the last 10 years.

In the map below, we see that the United States of America has by far the most UFO sightings.

Yearly UFO Sighting Trends

The line chart below shoes the trend of UFO sightings over the last 10 years.
We can see that generally, the number of UFO Sightings increases
as the years progress. Interestingly, we see a number of spikes in UFO reports.
The alien emojis on the line chart correspond to the 4th of July, which is the United States' Independence Day.
The number of UFO sightings tend to spike during the 4th of July.
This may be caused by excessive use of fireworks, leading people to see flashing lights in the sky.
Alternatively, excessive consumption of alcohol on the 4th of July may impair judgement.
Intoxicated people may be misinterpreting airplanes or lasers in the sky as UFO sightings, explaining the report spikes
during the 4th of July.

Most Referenced Animals during UFO Sighting Reports

A classic UFO folk tale involves a large flying saucer abducting a cow from a field.
Looking at the data, however, shows that dogs are the most mentioned animal during UFO sightings,
with over 120 unique mentions. Birds are the next most mentioned animal, with around 40.
The iconic cow is only mentioned 5 times over the last 10 years of UFO sightings.
